We want to share information about common symptoms that may be related to a lung disease but remember that this is only to provide you with increased understanding. Only a doctor can make an accurate diagnosis. Please note that not all symptoms are serious, and some may resolve on their own, while others may need evaluation by a doctor.

Cough

Coughing is a way for the airways to try to get rid of things that irritate them. Most often, the cough is due to a cold and then it goes away by itself, although it can take several weeks. Coughing can also be caused by other diseases of the airways or lungs, or by irritants such as smoke. Coughing up blood should always be evaluated by a doctor. An infection is often the cause, but other conditions should be ruled out.

Shortness of breath

Shortness of breath may not be anything to worry about, but sometimes it can be serious and require medical help. Common causes include asthma, respiratory tract infections like pneumonia, obesity, and smoking. It can also be a sign of a panic attack. However, in some cases, it may indicate something more serious, such as a lung condition called chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) or lung cancer.

Wheezing

Wheezing is the sound you hear when your airway is partially blocked. This blockage can result from various conditions such as allergic reactions, colds, bronchitis, or allergies. Additionally, wheezing can be a symptom of asthma, pneumonia, heart failure, and other conditions. It may resolve on its own, but it can also indicate a serious underlying condition.

Hoarseness

When you're hoarse, your voice doesn't sound as it normally does. It's common to experience hoarseness with a cold, but it can also result from overstraining your voice. In most cases, the problem resolves on its own.

Swollen lymph nodes

Swollen lymph nodes are common. They might feel tender and bigger than usual. This happens when your immune system activates to defend your body against viruses and bacteria. It is recommended to have swollen lymph nodes evaluated by a doctor if they accompany an infected wound, if they are larger than 1.5 centimeters in size without ongoing severe throat infection, if they feel firm or fixed, or if you have swollen lymph nodes in multiple areas of your body along with symptoms such as pallor, fatigue or fever.

Repeated or persistent chest infections

Respiratory infections, such as bronchitis and pneumonia, affect your airways and lungs. Pneumonia is typically caused by bacteria, which is treated with antibiotics. If you experience frequent episodes of these infections, it could indicate an underlying health issue.

Chest or shoulder pain

Chest and shoulder pain can have various causes. It can for example be due to gallstones, angina pectoris or other heart diseases. It also may result from inflammation of the pericardium (pericarditis), the pleura (pleuritis) or the pancreas (pancreatitis). If you're experiencing unexplained pain in your chest or shoulder that persists, it's important to visit your doctor for evaluation.
